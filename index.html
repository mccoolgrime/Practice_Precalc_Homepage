Speaking in Precalculus

Mathematics is a language that both describes and shapes powerful stories about the world around us. Precalculus is an immersion program. Our goal here is to give you the skills to be successful anywhere the language of mathematics is spoken or written.
In this course, we will:
Predict the slant asymptote of a rational runction using polynomial long division.
Calculate the finite sum of an infinite sequence by hand and using technology.
Animate parametric equations to create artistic designs in the desmos graphing calcultor.
Determine the height of a hot air balloon using triangle trigonometry.

[T]he crux of the matter is the move from ... ordinary language to regimented language...[A]ll of math and all of science really gets off the ground by regimented language...So that’s at the heart of this...[Students] are actually looking at events and figuring out how to talk about those events in their own language. And so here, it doesn’t matter that you don’t speak the King’s English, right. It’s not the King’s English that really underlies the mathematics, right. It’s the regimented language, which is nobody’s.
